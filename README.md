### Project Overview

A simple tool to track, categorize and visualize organization expenses, helping to manage their budget effectively. Features include adding and editing transactions, generating summaries, and providing insights into spending patterns for smarter financial decisions.

### Data Cleaning and Preparation Process

- Imported raw transaction data into Excel and formatted it as a structured table.
- Removed duplicate entries and handled missing values appropriately.
- Standardized data types (dates and numeric amounts) and cleaned text fields for consistency.
- Created additional columns (Month, Year, Month-Year) to support time-based analysis.
- Applied data validation and reviewed outliers to ensure accuracy and reliability.
  
 Final Outcome: 
The dataset was successfully cleaned, standardized, and structured for analysis. The prepared data enables accurate expense categorization, trend analysis, and summary reporting using Pivot Tables and charts.
   

### Exploratory Data Analysis

 The exploratory data analysis was conducted to understand spending patterns, identify trends, and uncover key insights within the transaction dataset.
 - Total Spending Analysis: Calculated overall expenditure to determine total money spent within the selected time frame.
 - Category-wise Breakdown: Used Pivot Tables to analyze total and average spending per category, identifying the highest and lowest expense areas.
 - Monthly Trend Analysis: Grouped transactions by month to observe spending trends and detect increases or decreases over time.
 - Top Transactions Review: Sorted transactions by amount to identify high-value expenses and potential outliers.
 - Spending Frequency Analysis: Evaluated how often expenses occur across categories to detect recurring patterns.

Key Insights:
The analysis revealed dominant spending categories, recurring expense patterns, and variations in monthly expenditure, providing actionable insights for better budgeting and financial decision-making.

### Insight 

The data highlights two main financial control issues:
  1.  Lack of Expense Classification.
       - Nearly 23% of transactions are uncategorized.
       - Over $22,000 lacks preoper visibility.
       - This reduces the ability to monitor budgets effectively.
    
  2.  High Concentration in Daily Spending.
     - Nearly half of all spending (47%) comes from routine categories.
     - Small daily eexpenses collectively create large financial impact.
     - Even a small Percentage reduction could result in significant 
savings.

For example: 
   - A 10% reduction in Food, Coffee, Shopping and Transport
     = Approximately $4,622 in potential savings.

 
 ### Recommendation

 1. Reclassify Uncategorized Expenses
    - Target reduction from 23% to below 5%.
    - Improve reporting accuracy and transparency.
   
 2. Set spending limits for high-frequency categories
      - Focus on Food, Coffee, Shopping and Transport(47% of total spend).
   
 3. Review Subscriptions and recurring costs
    - Assess value for money.
    - Cancel unused services.
   
 4. Implement monthly Expense Reviews
    - Detect overspending early.
    - Strenghen budgeting and financial planning.
